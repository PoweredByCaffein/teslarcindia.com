SOME OF OUR RESEARCH WORKS


GRID GENERATION

High quality grids are essential for accurate numerical solution, since the grid metrics substantially affect the numerical solution. Orthogonal grids provide the solution closest to the physical one. In house code is developed for generation of such grids. Many a times, flow past cylinders, symmetric and cambered airfoils are simulated, which need orthogonal grids for accurate solution. We have expertise in generating orthogonal grids for such 2D geometries.


LOW Re FLOWS

The low Re flows displays a peculiar behavior. At lower Re and/or higher AoA, the shear layer fails to re-attach to the airfoil surface, while at higher Re and/or lower AoA, re-attachment may occur, leading to the formation of a separation bubble. The formation of separation bubble depends upon Re, AoA, and geometry of the airfoil. At very low Re the flow may not re-attach after separation.


TURBULENCE MODELING

In every practical applications the flow is unsteady, with varying intensities of turbulence. For any applications, the source of these background noises may be acoustic, vortical or entropic in origin. Hence, it is imperative that there is need for modeling omnipresent free stream turbulence (FST) accurately. We have developed a FST model using moving average method. The model is suitable for inducting FST for 2D and 3D flows. The FST generated using this model has been compared tested with the actual wind tunnel data of various wind tunnels and this model provides almost overlapping disturbance velocities.


UNSTEADY AERODYNAMICS

Unsteady flows at low Re have created significant interest in research due to their importance in engineering devices and in nature. Low Re flows display massive boundary layer separation, flow unsteadiness and bypass transition. Due to large vortical structures associated, simplified theories fall insufficient to explain the flow dynamics in such flows. Such flows are highly dominated by viscous force and steady-state aerodynamics is of limited values. To incorporate unsteady viscous flow features of separation and transition in the presence of large vortices, CFD techniques have been used to study flapping flight.


FLOW RECEPTIVITY

3D flow inside a square duct is a canonical flow with very specific flow properties, in the near absence of free stream turbulence (FST). Along with the geometrical symmetry, the flow topology when viewed in its equilibrium state also displays striking symmetries. Imposition of disturbances affect these symmetries differently depending upon the qualitative nature of the disturbance field. Thus, loss of symmetry is synonymous with instability of the flow field. This in turn also implies the receptivity of the flow field for different imposed disturbances. Apart from deterministic excitation near the wall, one is also interested in studying the effects of FST on receptivity and instability of the flow field.


FREE STREAM TURBULENCE

Understanding the nature of flow transition from laminar to turbulent is of critical importance for any fluid dynamical system. When a laminar flow is receptive to small background disturbances, it undergoes disturbance growth that might lead to transition. In nature, disturbances are omnipresent and their role in destabilizing fluid flow to an early turbulence is of great practical importance. These disturbances can arise from surface perturbations, free-stream noises, surface vibrations etc. For the flows with lower Reynolds number (Re), presence of a thicker boundary layer accelerates the effects of FST and hence such low Re flows are very suitable for study of FST.
